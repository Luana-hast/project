01 / The Origen

FromOlympics Velodrome
to House of Life

The story begins at the 1976 Montreal Olympic GamesThe building was designed by architectRoger Taillibertas an Olympic velodrome — its shape inspired by a fossilized trilobite shell.

In1988, Pierre Bourque proposed transforming the velodrome into a"biodôme"— a "house of life." After 3 years of work, the Biodôme opened on June 18, 1992, for the 350th anniversary of Montreal's founding. ,

03 / The Renovation

Reinvented in2020
— More Imersive Than Ever

After 2 year of renovation, the Biodôme reopened completely transformed. New pathways through the Tropical Rainforest and Laurentian Forest, along with anice tunnelthat transports visitors directly to the polar world of the penguins.

A new upper mezzanine offers a privileged view of three ecosystems at once. The Biodôme also launched anaugmented reality app featuring virtual polar bears, belugas, and jaguars.

  • →New elevated walkways in the Tropical and Laurentian forests
  • →Immersive ice tunnel for the Sub-Antarctic Islands
